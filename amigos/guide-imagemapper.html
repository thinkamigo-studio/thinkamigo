Imagemapper studio

User guide

The Imagemapper studio lets you define clickable regions — hotspots — on any image. It generates the code you need to make those regions interactive on a web page. No coding knowledge required to use the studio itself.

What it's for. Defining interactive areas on comic pages, trail maps, diagrams, or any image where you want a click or tap to take the reader somewhere.

Part 1 — Loading an image

  1. 1

    Open the studio

    Open imagemapper.html in your browser. You will see a dark canvas area on the left and a control panel on the right.

  2. 2

    Load your image

    Click Load image in the panel, or drag your image file directly onto the dark canvas. JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP and SVG files are all supported.

    The image will appear in the canvas at full size. Portrait images — such as comic pages — will be tall. Scroll down to see the full image.

Part 2 — Adding a marker image (optional)

  1. 3

    Upload a marker PNG

    In the Marker image section of the panel, click Upload PNG marker and choose your star or icon file. A small preview will appear.

    This is optional. Skip to step 4 if you don't need a visible marker on your hotspots.

  2. 4

    Place the file on your server

    Whatever PNG you upload for preview, the generated code will always reference it as assets/img/hotspot-marker.png. Place your chosen file at that path on your web server.

Part 3 — Placing hotspots

  1. 5

    Click Add hotspot

    Click Add hotspot in the Mapping section. An orange banner appears across the top of the canvas — you are now in mapping mode. A dashed rectangle appears centred on the image.

  2. 6

    Position the marquee

    Click and drag the rectangle to move it over the area you want to make clickable. In most cases, simply moving it into position is all you need to do.

    If you need a larger or smaller hotspot, drag any of the four corner handles to resize.

  3. 7

    Place the hotspot

    Click Place hotspot here. The hotspot is confirmed and added to the list on the right. It is named automatically — hotspot-01, hotspot-02, and so on.

    The hotspot will appear as a circle on your finished page, inscribed within the rectangle you drew.

  4. 8

    Show marker (optional)

    If you uploaded a marker image, tick the marker checkbox next to the hotspot in the list. The marker PNG will appear centred over the hotspot in the generated code.

  5. 9

    Add more hotspots

    Click Add hotspot again to place another. Repeat as many times as needed. Each one is added to the list and numbered in sequence.

  6. 10

    Adjust a hotspot

    Click any hotspot in the list to select it. Its marquee becomes active again — drag to reposition it, or drag the corners to resize. Changes are applied immediately.

    To remove a hotspot, click the ✕ button next to it in the list.

Part 4 — Generating the code

  1. 11

    Click Generate embedding code

    Click the Generate embedding code button at the bottom of the panel. A window opens with three tabs.

HTML tab

The image and hotspot map. Paste this into your page <body> where the image should appear.

CSS tab

The styles for the hotspot overlays. Paste into a <style> block in your page <head> or your stylesheet.

JavaScript tab

The responsive positioning script. Paste before the closing </body> tag, wrapped in a <script> tag.

  1. 12

    Copy each tab to BBEdit

    Click Copy to clipboard on each tab in turn and paste into your page in BBEdit. The tab note tells you exactly where each block goes.

  2. 13

    Replace the placeholder URLs

    Each hotspot has href="#" as a placeholder. Replace the # with the actual URL or page you want the hotspot to link to.

Starting a new image. Click Close session to clear the canvas and start fresh with a different image. All hotspots are cleared. The studio is ready to load again immediately.
