David Amos - Mining historian and community researcher

David Amos

David and I share a starting point that makes the partnership unusual. Both from mining families, David went down the pit. I received an honorary membership of the National Union Of Mineworks and a coal industry scholarship to study art. Our paths diverged. We found each other decades later at a Digital Ambassadors presentation and later ended up creating something neither of us could do alone.

David brings what no platform or design methodology can manufacture — academic credibility earned from the inside, and the kind of community trust that only comes from belonging to the world you are documenting. A former pit worker, doctoral researcher and mining historian, he has spent years capturing stories that were in danger of disappearing, and steering an impartial path through a contested history of industrial comnflict with the generation that lived it.

A History of Coal Mining in 10 Objects — AHRC funded, involving the Universities of Nottingham, Leicester and Nottingham Trent — explored mining culture and working processes through iconography. The resulting website combined with an illustrated booklet, public presentations and public video screenings established an accessible format that was perfect for community outreach. Shortly after this project we co-founded a community interest company (CIC) Mine2Minds and the projects that have followed represent more than a decade of sustained commitment to the former mineworkers and communities of the East Midlands region.

Coal, Community and Change - a touring photgraphic exhibition assembled with Nottingham Trent University, reached over 70,000 visitors over the course of six months. Banners and Beyond, published by Thinkamigo Editions in 2023, examined the union banner tradition across the coalfield. David has also advised theatrical productions, filmmakers, academic researchers and broadcasters — including Jeremy Paxman during his primary research for Black Gold — a measure of the authority of Mine2Minds.

Coaltime Days, a new book by David, with design by Thinkamigo imprint, is due to be published, summer 2026.

Paul Fillingham
