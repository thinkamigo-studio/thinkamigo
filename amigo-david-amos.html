David Amos - Mining historian and community researcher

Mining historian and community researcher

David and I share a starting point that makes the partnership unusual. He went down the pit. I received a coal industry scholarship to study art. Our paths diverged. We found each other decades later with something neither of us could do alone.

David brings what no platform or design methodology can manufacture — academic credibility earned from the inside, and the kind of community trust that only comes from belonging to the world you are documenting. A former pit worker, doctoral researcher and mining historian, he has spent years recovering histories that were in danger of disappearing with the generation that lived them.

We co-founded Mine2Minds CIC together, and the projects that have followed represent more than a decade of sustained commitment to the Nottinghamshire coalfield. A History of Coal Mining in 10 Objects — AHRC funded, with the Universities of Nottingham, Leicester and Nottingham Trent — established the methodology: objects as portals into a world, each carrying histories their owners didn't always recognise. On screen, David's knowledge of the hierarchies and systems of the coalfield ground every frame.

Coal, Community and Change, assembled with Nottingham Trent University, reached over 70,000 visitors in six months. Banners and Beyond, published by Thinkamigo Editions in 2023, examined the union banner tradition across the coalfield. David has also assisted serious researchers and broadcasters — including Jeremy Paxman during primary research for Black Gold — a measure of the credibility this body of work has built.

Coaltime Days, a new publication written by David and designed by Thinkamigo, is forthcoming.

Paul Fillingham
