EARTH framework

Five interconnected domains — Enterprise, Arts, Research, Training, Heritage — organised around a single creative feedback loop. No domain operates in isolation. Select any domain to read more.

Studio thinkamigo — thinkOS

The through-line

Studio thinkamigo activities are organised across five propositions. EARTH is the map of the territory — what the studio actually does, as distinct from how it is presented and priced through the brand taxonomy.

No project sits cleanly inside one domain. No domain operates in isolation. The through-line is storytelling. The creative feedback loop turns continuously — each domain informing and enriching the others.

thinkOS is the connective tissue. The methodology that runs beneath all five domains and makes the practice coherent across them.

E — Enterprise

Commercial practice

Four decades of professional practice across government digital services, international commercial clients, broadcast post-production and early web development.

Current work includes contracting through Hanover Consulting to HMRC on the Multichannel Digital Tax Platform, GDS Research, Accessibility and Design assessor since 2021, and the think iS. design intelligence proposition.

Enterprise operates across two registers: UK government digital transformation (GDS framework, accessibility standards, user research at scale) and international commercial practice (Harneys Digital Branding Gold Award, Living Group).

The rigour of government service design sharpens the creative practice. The lateral thinking of the Arts domain makes the professional work distinctive in a field that defaults to process over insight.

A — Arts

Creative practice

The domain where the artist drive finds its fullest expression — an active practitioner, not a service provider to artists. Validated by Arts Council England funding through collaborations combining creative vision with production capability.

The producer/creator duality is central: platform, audience, distribution and resources as producer; artistic drive, vision and the need to make as creator.

Active projects: Houdini Window (novel, ~90,000 words, near completion), Seaside Sci-Fi (transmedia platform, Morecambe Bay), Memory Theatre (D.H. Lawrence, NTU), Whatever People Say I Am (digital comics, NTU), Dawn of the Unread (Guardian Award, 2015).

R — Research

Evidence-based practice

Currently anchored in Government Digital Service methodology, embedded within project teams inside UK government agencies. Seven years across two HMRC programmes through Capgemini and Equal Experts.

The discipline demanded — evidence-based decision making, structured research methodologies, stakeholder navigation in complex organisations — feeds directly into the creative feedback loop across all five domains.

Academic and heritage research runs in parallel: AHRC collaboration with Dr David Amos (A History of Coal Mining in 10 Objects, 2013), NTU collaborations across Memory Theatre and Whatever People Say I Am.

T — Training

Untapped revenue stream

A domain in its own right — latent but with strong foundations across all five EARTH sectors. Every methodology developed across four decades is teachable: Six Sombreros, connections thinking, transmedia production pipeline, open source platform development, GDS service design.

Three formats: face-to-face workshops; remote delivery with downloadable resources; embedded training within funded projects where the production process itself becomes the curriculum.

The thinkIS Learn self-serve course is the scalable expression — no direct time required once built. The Seaside Sci-Fi model cuts across Arts, Heritage and Training simultaneously.

H — Heritage

Personal and professional convergence

The domain where personal and professional converge most completely. The background — mining village, counterculture, Fine Art formation — is not separate from the heritage work. It is the heritage work.

Five strands: Mine2Minds CIC (coalfield regeneration, est. 2015 with Dr David Amos); MyTrail (location-aware digital trails — Robin Hood, D.H. Lawrence, Pleasley Pit); thinkamigoEDITIONS (eleven publications, British Library registered); print heritage testimony; heritage workshops (museum curators, community groups, academic institutions).

Heritage feeds everything else. Iconographic research feeds EDITIONS. Community connections feed Arts Council bids. The trails strand feeds Training workshops. Mine2Minds reframed around green transition has a funding future that the elegiac heritage model does not.

The through-line is storytelling. No project sits cleanly inside one domain. The Fine Art formation and the professional practice exist in a symbiotic relationship — each continuously informing and enriching the other through a creative feedback loop that has never stopped turning.
