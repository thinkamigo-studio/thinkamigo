Paul Fillingham

Digital producer

UX research, platform design, creative direction

paul@thinkamigo.com

Award-winning digital producer with experience across government services, ecommerce, arts and heritage. Working at the leading edge of every significant shift in digital practice — not as an observer but as an early adopter and practitioner in each successive wave. The consistent skill across every project being platform thinking — combining content, architecture and engagement.

Currently contracting inside UK Government an advocate of Government Digital Service (GDS) with experience of several departments, including HMRC, DVSA, DfE, the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority, the National Resilience Extranet — applying Agile, accessible, user-centred design methods across some of the most complex government services in existence. Services used by 40 million people. An HMRC Peoples Award nomination in 2021 and a Digital Branding Gold Award for Harneys Europe and Asia sit alongside that work.

A career that moved through every wave of digital change as it arrived — art direction in the agency years, then ecommerce when that was still a new idea, then digital arts and heritage platforms. Photoshop since 1989, HTML since 1994. At Headland Multimedia, took a national DIY retailer from no web presence at all to £6.4 million in annual online turnover. Founded Thinkamigo Ltd in 2012.

Arts and heritage work runs alongside commerce and government. The Sillitoe Trail — selected as one of 50 projects for the BBC and Arts Council England's on-demand digital arts platform, The Space.

The Guardian award-winning digital comic Dawn of the Unread. The digital storytelling platform has formed the basis of ongoing learning projects with Nottingham Trent University, helping secure Nottingham's status as a UNESCO City of Literature.

Industrial heritage work with Mine2Minds — co-founded with mining historian Dr David Amos — and Nottingham Trent University including Coal, Community and Change — an exhibition that reached over 70,000 visitors in six months. BBC appearances across television and national radio. An AI-assisted production pipeline now running for Houdini Window, a cold war thriller and first novel.
